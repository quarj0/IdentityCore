# IdentityCore Product Strategy

**Version:** 1.0

---

## Purpose

This document defines the long-term product strategy for IdentityCore.

It explains:

- What IdentityCore is.
- Why it exists.
- Who it serves.
- Why organizations should choose it.
- How the platform will evolve.
- What is intentionally out of scope.

This document guides product decisions rather than technical implementation.

---

## Product Positioning

IdentityCore is an enterprise digital identity infrastructure platform.

Version 1.0 delivers secure identity verification, but the platform is designed to become the trusted identity layer for governments, enterprises, and digital services.

IdentityCore is not simply another KYC product.

It is infrastructure.

Just as Stripe provides payment infrastructure, IdentityCore aims to provide identity infrastructure.

---

## Product Vision

To become the trusted identity platform powering secure digital interactions across Africa.

IdentityCore enables organizations to establish trust between people, systems, and digital services through secure, privacy-first, and interoperable identity technologies.

---

## Market Opportunity

Many organizations struggle with fragmented identity systems.

Current challenges include:

- Manual verification
- Multiple disconnected vendors
- Expensive verification services
- Poor interoperability
- Vendor lock-in
- Limited ownership of identity data
- Inconsistent verification standards
- Difficult government integrations

These challenges create an opportunity for a unified identity platform.

## Product Access Models

IdentityCore supports two access models.

### Developer Integration

For technical organizations that want to embed IdentityCore directly into their own systems.

Includes:

- REST APIs
- API Clients
- Webhooks
- SDKs
- Developer documentation

### No-Code Hosted Verification

For organizations that want to verify people without building a custom integration.

Includes:

- Dashboard-based verification creation
- Hosted verification links
- Verification policy builder
- Manual review interface
- Reports and exports

Both access models use the same underlying verification infrastructure.

IdentityCore is not:

- A social login provider
- A consumer identity platform
- A payment gateway
- A CRM
